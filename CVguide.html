Guide to Creating a CV

Learn how to make your cv more presentable and eye-catching for your employers. At Jobhives, we create a secure environment to support sutudents with making the perfect cv.


Different Types of CV

There are differnet types of CV's which include:

  • Academic CV
  • Skill-based CV

Customizing Your CV to Desired Job Vacancies

Conduct Detailed Research. Thorough understanding of the job description of specific job to know how your skills, knowledge and experience can be applied to the employers taste.
Transferable Skills which are not too relevant to the job being applied for but can be used in work/office environment. Skills such as Listening skills, Communication skills, etc.
Remove Irrelevant Points. Remove irrelevant points on your cv that do not relate to the job description you are applying for.

Important Information to Create a Perfect CV

Depending on the job you are applying for, the following points can be used to create the perfect cv. You may choose to include some of the points or exclude them, it is up to you.
These points include:

  1. Name and Contact Details: use your name as the header of the page and make sure the font is big and bold. Do not include your date of birth or personal information in your cv.
  2. Profile: Use about 4 to 5 line to talk about yourself in terms of what you offer, your experience and other attributes you can bring to the job being applied for.
  3. Key Skills: This is where you add the relevant skills needed for your Job based on your academic status. For example, a Computer Science student skills can be C#, C++, Javascript, etc.
  4. Education: present your educational qualification starting with your latest educational program which should include your degree. Provide dates of qualification, grades, predicted grades and the names of the school you attended. Also provide relevant information about skills gain in this period and how it can be transferred to the job you are applying for.
  5. Employment History: for individuals with previous jobs, give full details of your previous employment. Give the companies name and you role in the company. Also remember to add transferrable skills from your previous job to attract the current employer.
  6. Addition Activities/Interests: talk about extra curricular activities that you have partaken in while at school such as sports, media, music, social committee roles, etc.
  7. Referees: you can decide to include this section or not. If you do, you should provide at least two referees. One is from your course like Personal Supervisor or someone from family. Write their name, organisation, workplace address, job title, etc. If you don not want to provide these details, you can say 'Reference Available on Request' on your page footer.
